On this day in 1944, 730 delegates from 44 nations gathered at the Mount Washington Hotel in Bretton Woods, New Hampshire, for a meeting that would redefine the global financial landscape.

As we mark July 1 today, we look back at the start of the Bretton Woods Conference. In the midst of World War II, international leaders sought to establish a new monetary system to ensure global economic stability and prevent the protectionist policies that had exacerbated the Great Depression. This landmark summit led to the creation of the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the International Bank for Reconstruction and Development, now part of the World Bank Group.

The frameworks established during this conference laid the groundwork for modern global trade, cross-border investment, and the financial cooperation that continues to underpin our international economy.
